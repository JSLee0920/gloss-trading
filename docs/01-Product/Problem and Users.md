# Problem and Users

## The three failures

**Market understanding.** "Price dropped 5% and I don't know why. By the time I find the news the move is over."

**Behavioural blindness.** "I always trade badly when I'm recovering losses. I wish I'd seen the pattern earlier." The pattern is visible in the data long before it is visible to the trader.

**Generic education.** "Half the videos were too basic, half too advanced, none matched what I actually trade."

## Primary user

A retail trader with 3+ months of history on at least one instrument, who has lost money in ways they cannot articulate, and who can export a statement from their broker.

Not a beginner with no trades — the product has nothing to work with. Onboarding requires history.

## The competing product

YouTube, generic courses, and trading journals. Gloss beats them on one axis only: **it uses your trades, not someone else's examples.** Every design decision should defend that axis.

## Onboarding promise

Drop in a statement, and within one screen see one detected pattern, one replayed trade, one lesson. If that first 60 seconds does not land, nothing after it matters. See [[Phases]].
